The best writing advice I've encountered is also the most counterintuitive: write for yourself first.

Not because your audience doesn't matter. But because clarity of thinking precedes clarity of expression. If you don't know what you're trying to say, no amount of editing will fix it.

**Explanation is compression.** When I try to write something and I can't make it concise, it usually means I don't understand it well enough yet. The writing is revealing my ignorance, not just recording it.

This is why I'm starting this log. Not to publish essays. Not to build an audience (at least not yet). But to force a daily compression exercise — take what I observed or read or thought today, and compress it into something I could explain to a smart person over coffee.

If it's not explainable, I go back and think harder.

That's the habit I'm building.
