A feature solves a problem. A product creates a habit.

Most things shipped as "products" are features in disguise. They fix a specific pain, but they don't build a loop. Users come when they have the problem and leave when it's solved.

A real product is something people return to — not because they have to, but because it's now part of how they operate.

**Why this distinction matters in credit:**

Most credit apps are feature-level. Disbursement is fast, EMI reminders go out, balance is visible. But where's the habit? Where's the reason to open the app when you're not transacting?

The products that win in credit will be the ones that find a reason for the user to return — whether that's a credit health score, a financial coaching nudge, or a savings product tied to repayment behavior.

A loan is a feature. A financial relationship is a product.
