## The concept

Clayton Christensen's Jobs To Be Done (JTBD) reframes the question from *"who is the customer?"* to *"what job is the customer hiring this product to do?"*

People don't buy products. They hire them to make progress in their lives.

## Why it matters for credit

A personal loan isn't hired because someone needs ₹2 lakhs. It's hired because:
- Someone needs to feel in control of a cash flow gap
- Someone needs to not ask family for money (dignity)
- Someone needs to close a financial emergency without panic

The functional job (get money) is obvious. The emotional and social jobs are where differentiation lives — and where most credit products fail.

## My take

Most Indian fintech credit products are optimized for the functional job (fast disbursal, low friction). Very few are designed for the emotional job. That's the gap.

## Use this when

Whenever you're designing onboarding, rejection flows, or repayment UX — ask what emotional job the user is trying to complete at that moment, not just the functional one.
